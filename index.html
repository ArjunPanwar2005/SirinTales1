Types of Harassment

Harassment is any unwanted behavior, physical or verbal (or even suggested), that makes a reasonable person feel uncomfortable, humiliated, or mentally distressed. Harassment covers a wide range of unwanted behavior, including physical contact and verbal abuse that causes emotional distress to the harassed

Examples of verbal harassment:

1. Suggestive or insulting sounds such as whistling, wolf calls

2. comments about the sensuality of a person, or his/her spouse or significant other

3. distribution or display of written or graphic materials that are derogatory and of a sexual nature

4. comments about a person’s clothing, body, and/or sexual behaviors or sexual orientation

See More

Examples of unwanted physical contact

1. Touching that is inappropriate in the workplace or classroom, such as patting, pinching, stroking, groping.

2. Giving a massage around the neck or shoulders

3. Intentionally blocking another person’s path and physical assault.

See More

Examples of online/technology abuse

1. monitoring a person’s whereabouts using technological means (social media, gps tracking, etc.) and using this information to harass or stalk

2. name-calling, trolling, threats, or stalking conducted online

3. Putting inappropriate comments on social media for a specific person.
